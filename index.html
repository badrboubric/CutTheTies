CUT THE TIES!

The academic community urges Dutch universities to cut ties with the fossil fuel industry due to their history of undermining science and research bias through sponsorship activities, which contradicts their commitment to unbiased research and a just world.

Study Associations


Multiple study associations are sponsored by the fossil fuel giants, Shell and/or ExxonMobil and in some cases organize excursions to sites of these companies.

Student theses and -projects at Shell and multiple Shell employees are invited for guest lectures

Multiple professors and other UT staff have roles and are affiliated or are working for Shell.

Alumni

The UT Ambassador Network consists of more than 70 alumni and relations who fill leading positions in the corporate world, governments, or Twente startups. Many of these alumni reportedly are working or have worked for Shell or ExxonMobil.

Awards

The UT and its students have participated in awards and marathons that have been organized by Shell. At the UT entrepreneurial challenge, Shell hands out the Shell Energy Transition Awards, and the Green Team of the UT has for many years participated in the Shell Eco Marathon.

Research

Fossil fuel companies fund professor positions and many research projects
